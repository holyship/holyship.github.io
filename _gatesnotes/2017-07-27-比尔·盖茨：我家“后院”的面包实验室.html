我去过很多实验室，但直到最近才第一次见到一个以全功能厨房为核心的实验室。上个月，我得到了拜访“面包实验室”的机会，它位于我家乡西雅图的北面，开车过去大概要几个小时。实验室里聚集了当地的农民、育种专家和世界级的烘焙师，他们的目标是要生产出适合制作烘焙食品的小麦，使得这些烘焙食品既美味又健康。



踏入实验室大门的那一刻，我就闻到了烤面包的香味。靠墙放着一罐罐用各种不同品种小麦磨成的面粉，烘焙师傅正在从大型商用烤箱中取出一个个热气腾腾的面包。在实验室的角落里，我看到一台用来测量面团筋道程度的粉质仪。


一切都令人印象深刻，但我内心深处还是有一点点怀疑：这些面包究竟味道如何？面包实验室重点向我介绍了全麦面包，实验室主任史蒂夫‧琼斯（Steve Jones）还推荐了他个人十分喜欢的荞麦面包，因为荞麦通常含有更加丰富的赖氨酸和其他营养物质。虽然吃全麦面包可能会让我觉得自己吃得很健康，但是根据我的经验，全麦面包始终不如白面包好吃。


但在品尝了他们烘焙的许多产品之后，我很高兴地告诉大家：我在这里吃的所有东西都很美味，就连很难用全麦面粉做好的羊角面包都很好吃。


面包实验室隶属于华盛顿州立大学。虽然他们大部分的工作都是与发达国家的公司合作——包括克里夫巴尔（Clif Bar）、拉布瑞面包店（La Brea Bakery）和 Chipotle 快餐店——但他们也在将自己的专业技术应用于解决贫困国家营养不良的问题上。例如我在这里遇到的来自巴基斯坦的博士生法拉赫‧艾哈迈德（Farah Ahmad），她正尝试利用面包实验室研发出的技术制作薄饼、饼干和其他来自她家乡的食品。



The Bread Lab in My Backyard


I’ve been to lots of labs, but until recently I had never seen onethat was centered on a full working kitchen. Last month I got my chance when Ivisited the Bread Lab, located acouple hours north of my hometown Seattle. It brings together local farmers,expert plant breeders, and world-class bakers with the goal of producing wheatfor baked goods that are both delicious and good for you.


I could smell the baking bread the minute I walked in the door. Canistersof flour from different types of wheat lined the walls. Bakers were pullingwarm rolls out of large commercial ovens. In the corner there was afarinograph, a machine that measures the strength of dough.


It was all very impressive. But deep down, I was a little skeptical: How would the breads actually taste? The Bread Lab emphasizes using wholegrains, and not just wheat—lab director Steve Jones loves buckwheat becauseit’s unusually high in lysine and other nutrients. Although eating whole-grainbread might make me feel virtuous, in my experience it just never seems totaste as good as white bread.


But I got to sample lots of their baked goods, and I’m happy toreport that everything I ate was delicious—even the croissants, which are especiallyhard to get right when you’re using whole grains.


The lab is part of Washington State University,and although most of their work is with rich-world companies including ClifBar, La Brea Bakery, and Chipotle, they are also applying their expertise tothe problem of malnutrition in poorer countries. For example I met a Pakistani Ph.D.student, Farah Ahmad, who’s looking at ways to make chapatis, cookies, andother foods from her home country using approaches developed by the Bread Lab.
