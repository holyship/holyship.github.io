写给偶像Goetz:迟到10年的奖励

文丨飞总  图丨源自网络

SIGMOD是ACM下面负责Database相关的组织。其每年召开的SIGMOD会议是数据库领域最顶级的会议之一，另外一个和它并列的是VLDB。今年是SIGMOD的多事之秋。在经历了因为北卡州对LGBT不友好而临时换开会地点风波，和Trump对若干国家签证禁令以后又考虑是否换到美国以外开会之后，SIGMOD2017终于最终定在了芝加哥。

SIGMOD的一则新闻引起了我的注意。Goetz Graefe获得了今年SIGMOD的Innovations Award。Innovations Award授予给在数据库领域做出奠基性工作的人。这个奖在关系数据库的发明人Edgar Codd去世以后为了纪念他就加上了他的名字来命名了。

今年获奖的人，并不是在数据库领域要风得风要雨得雨的那种大山一般的人物。我不知道都有谁知道他。但是在我接触关系数据库，开始读PhD以后，我接触到他对这个领域所作出的贡献，非常的崇拜此人的成就。在我个人偶像里，Goetz当之无愧的可以居前10。

我年年都在刷这个Innovation Award，总觉得Goetz这样贡献的人早就应该获得了，然而一直都没有发给他。一年又一年之后，我想，可能他这辈子就没希望了吧。然后慢慢的终于也就不关注了。那么这现年的奖都颁给谁了呢，我们可以看看。

这些获奖的人，有些当然是名至实归，还有一些，可能就需要打一点折扣了。我不知道大家到底认识几个。但是如果想对数据库领域的发展和现存的山头们有一个了解的话，这个图里面的每个人，其实都还是很值得去了解研究一下的。


关于Goeta的贡献，SIGMOD的得奖宣言是这样说的：

Professor Goetz Graefe is the recipient of the 2017 ACM SIGMOD Edgar F. Codd Innovations Award for his foundational contributions to the architecture and implementation of database query optimizers.

Details: Goetz Graefe has been a professor, product architect, and industrial researcher. He has published tutorial surveys on query execution, sorting, b-tree indexing, concurrency control and recovery as well as numerous novel techniques and research results in query processing and in transactional data storage. Like other database vendors, Microsoft SQL Server adopted his designs for query optimization and query execution.


我们对比一下，SIGMOD还有一个奖项Contributions Award。这个奖是给这个database research 社区做出贡献的人的，今年是Yannis。看看SIGMOD是怎么说他的：

Professor Yannis E. Ioannidis is the recipient of the 2017 ACM SIGMOD Contributions Award for his sustained leadership and dedicated service to the database community, especially as part of the SIGMOD Executive Committee and the VLDB Endowment.

Details: Yannis Ioannidis is currently a Professor at the Department of Informatics and Telecommunications of the University of Athens. In early 2011 he also became the President and General Director of the ATHENA Research and Innovation Center; in addition, he currently serves as the Acting Director of the Institute of Language and Speech Processing, the Industrial Systems Institute, and the Institute for the Management of Information Systems of ATHENA.

He received his Diploma in Electrical Engineering from the National Technical University of Athens in 1982, his MSc in Applied Mathematics from Harvard University in 1983, and his Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from the University of California-Berkeley in 1986. Immediately after that he joined the faculty of the Computer Sciences Department of the University of Wisconsin-Madison, where he became a Professor before finally leaving in 1999. His research interests include database and information systems, personalization and social networks, data infrastructures and digital libraries & repositories, scientific systems and workflows, eHealth systems, and human-computer interaction, topics on which he has published over one hundred articles in leading journals and conferences. He also holds three patents.

Yannis is an ACM and IEEE Fellow (2004 and 2010, respectively) and a member Academia Europaea. He is also a recipient of the VLDB “10-Year Best Paper Award” (2003), the “Presidential Young Investigator Award” – PYI (1991), and of several awards for teaching excellence, including the nation-wide “Xanthopoulos-Pneumatikos Award for Outstanding Academic Teaching” in Greece (2006) and the “Chancellor’s Award for Excellence in Teaching” at the University of Wisconsin (1996). He has also been a keynote or invited speaker in several conferences (NLDB’08, ICDE’07, ADBIS’06, CIKM’05, ICDT’03, WAIM’01, SSDBM’00, PDP’00, ECDL’98).

Yannis has been a (co-)principal investigator in over thirty five research projects funded by various government agencies (USA, Europe, Greece) or private industry. He is currently an Associate Editor of Information Systems and the Journal of Digital Libraries and has been a member of the program committees of over sixty conferences, six times as (co-)chair (ICDE’09, ADBIS’07, EDBT’06, HDMS’03, VLDB’02, VDB’98, and SSDBM’97).

Yannis has served as the ACM SIGMOD Chair (July 2009-June 2013), following a 4-year term as Vice-Chair, and is or has been a member of several other executive bodies of professional organizations (VLDB Endowment, IEEE TCDE Executive Committee, EDBT Endowment) and Scientific Advisory Boards (Max Planck Institute for Informatics, Greek National Science & Technology Council, Information Technology advisor to the Greek Minister of Health). He currently serves as the Greek delegate to the European Strategy Forum on Research Infrastructures (ESFRI), the ESFRI representative to the e-Infrastructures Reflection Group (e-IRG), and an expert in the Programme Committee on Research Infrastructures within the European Commission’s Horizon2020 Framework Programme.


咋一看Yannis还比Goetz牛逼多了。Goetz的贡献说得稀里糊涂的，用人话翻译一下呢，贡献很多很大，但是我觉得最大的有两个：

  1. Volcano Optimizer。这个是继System R的optimizer框架以后在框架上最为突破性的也可能是过去20年内唯一突破性的创新。

  2. 他总结了query execution的operator里面的open，next， close的范式，并提出了使用exchange operator来解决数据库查询执行时候的并发问题。这个框架和exchange operator统治了整个商业数据库10余年。最近几年才有所改变。


他对数据库系统的深刻理解从他早年的时候做的关于query execution的综述论文里面就可以感受到。时隔20多年，他的那篇综述依然是关系数据库系统入门必读的文献之一，也是我早年读过的对我影响最大的论文之一。

Goetz早年做professor，待过两个大学，后来他的Volcano optimizer卖给了微软就进工业界开发SQL Server。上世纪90年代末做到了微软的Tech Fellow。后来据说和SQL Server那边的人闹矛盾，之后就离开微软去HP养老了。

为什么Goetz获奖那么晚，SIGMOD给的detail又那么短呢？坊间传闻Goetz这个人情商很一般，和他导师以及数据库领域的“大牛”们的关系好像并不是那么的融洽。所以在这个讲关系讲圈子的数据库领域里面，就不一定获得待见了。

无论如何，我想Innovations还是要看Innovation的，不管怎么样，偶像得奖，终究是可喜可贺的一件事。
